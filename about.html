About Me

My Journey

I'm a Computer Science & Engineering student at American International University-Bangladesh (AIUB) with a deep appreciation for the craftsmanship behind well-built software. From my first line of code, I realized that great software isn't just about functionality — it's about reliability, user trust, and the invisible quality that makes an application feel seamless and effortless. This realization naturally led me to Software Quality Assurance, where I find genuine satisfaction in breaking down complex systems, identifying edge cases, and ensuring every component works exactly as intended. Whether designing test cases in Jira, tracking defects in Trello, or collaborating in Agile/Scrum teams, my approach is rooted in discipline, curiosity, and an unyielding standard for quality.

My developer's background gives me a unique perspective in testing. Through 8 academic projects building full-stack applications, desktop systems, and database-driven solutions with PHP, MySQL, Java, C#, and C++, I don't just know how to find bugs — I understand why they happen and how to prevent them. Beyond the classroom, I've represented AIUB at the 4th National Research Project Contest, earned certifications in Software Testing and Web Development, and actively participate in workshops and tech events, always seeking to grow and stay curious.

I'm currently seeking a Quality Assurance internship where I can bring my developer's perspective to testing, learn from experienced engineers, and contribute to products that users can trust. When I'm not testing or coding, I'm exploring new technologies, participating in coding competitions, or connecting with fellow tech enthusiasts — because I believe the best engineers never stop learning.
